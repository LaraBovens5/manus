Manual Therapy

What is manual therapy?

A manual therapist focuses on improving mobility, reducing pain, and enhancing posture. The emphasis is on identifying the root cause of the issue and understanding the body as a coherent whole, with special attention to the spine.

Manual therapy is relevant for various groups, including athletes.

Each treatment begins with a comprehensive movement assessment, evaluating joint mobility broadly and specifically. This optimizes both mobility and functionality, allowing joints to move correctly and reducing pain more effectively.

What can you see a manual therapist for?

  • Back pain with or without radiating symptoms
  • Neck pain with or without radiating symptoms
  • Headache and Jaw complaints
  • Whiplash
  • Hernia
  • Frozen shoulder
  • Pain and stiffness due to osteoarthritis and rheumatic conditions
  • Rib and chest complaints
  • General shoulder complaints
